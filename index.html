Letter · Application

Dear Ellen,

Once again, welcome to this application and thank you for taking the time to read it. Your listing made it really clear that you're someone who cares deeply, and my first thought was honestly "oh, she sounds lovely, I'd love to get to know her." So let me try to put into words who I am, to give you a better sense of who I'd be as a housemate ...

I'm creative at heart and a deeply caring person, about the people in my life, about the planet, about doing the small things that make others feel seen. I'm queer, I'm endlessly curious about art, music, ideas, languages and the way other people see the world, and I genuinely love learning new things, talking about them, and helping where I can. I have a lot of creative hobbies such as sewing, drawing and DIY-projects that are my way of recharging, getting out of my head and feel good. That being said I of course don't plan to build or do anything without consent, just to be clear. For me, my home is where I recharge, so I really value it being a calm, peaceful place. Somewhere I look forward to coming back to, and somewhere I take care of properly.

Right now I am physically in Copenhagen wrapping my life up until I move to the Netherlands at the start of May. I've lived in the Netherlands three times before, including eight months in Rotterdam on exchange at RSM, and over the years I've built a network here of both internationals and Dutch friends who feel like chosen family, and they are the main reason I am now relocating permanently to the Netherlands. Coming to Rotterdam feels like coming home, and I'm planning to stay long-term. This isn't a let's-try-it-out-move for me, it's more of a homecoming. I have a temporary place lined up at a friend's house, but I'm looking for a place to move into during May. (I can pay from May 5th, no problem).

As you maybe can imagine, moving abroad is not just an easy fix and right now I'm in the slightly chaotic in-between of a big move but this is something I have planned for a long time. In the beginning of this year, I finished my master's in human resource management at Copenhagen Business School. Even though studying at the echo chamber of Scandinavian finance bros may seem a bit off-brand, I like to see it myself as smashing the patriarchy from within, haha! The past year of my masters degree, I did a deep dive into the topic of inclusion of neurodivergent employees in office work to better understand how workplaces can be more inclusive, using theory about ableism and minority stress theory. This is a topic I could talk about for a long time so I will stop myself here, but it's safe to say that I am also always up for an interesting talk about similar topics. Ideally, diversity and inclusion is the work I want to spend my life on and what I am very passionate about.

My days at the moment are a mix of wrapping up my life in CPH, last coffees with friends here, and a sewing project I keep promising myself I'll finish before the move haha! My flight is scheduled to land in Schiphol on May 4th at 17:00 so I am available for online viewings all day during May 3rd or in person on May 4th in the evening. So where do I see myself in 3 months? By the end of July, I hope my mornings start with coffee, and that I am either settled into an HR role or similar and I dream about being able to come home to a place that is quiet, and where I can feel at peace, contribute to having a nice home and be around a creative and open person such as yourself to continue growing and learning and exploring in life!

I'm someone who deeply understands that people live and do things differently. I've spent a long time, both academically and personally, thinking about that. All I ask in return is honest, clear communication and genuine respect, and I'll bring the same. I think the small everyday things, like saying when something's bothering you, being considerate around quiet hours, and sharing the cleaning without keeping score, are what make a shared home feel safe rather than tense.

Thank you for writing such a thoughtful ad, by the way. That kind of care is rare, and it really does make a difference. I'd love to be invited to see the place either online or in person and answer any questions you might have.

– Cami 🌱
